When & Why We Use K-Fold Cross-Validation

Dataset Size Matters (The "Polling" Analogy)

Evaluating a model is exactly like conducting a political poll. Your Validation Set is the group of people you are polling to see if your hyperparameter (like $\lambda$) is good.

Large Datasets (e.g., 100,000 images)

If you hold out 20% for validation, you are polling 20,000 examples. Statistically, this is a massive sample size. The results will be highly stable and trustworthy. A single validation split is perfect here. K-Fold would just waste computation time.

Small Datasets (e.g., Sonar Dataset)

The Sonar dataset only has ~200 rows. A 20% split means polling just 40 examples. It is very easy to randomly pick 40 "easy" examples or 40 "hard" outliers by pure luck. A single split is a massive gamble. We MUST use K-Fold to average out this statistical variance.

Mapping it to your Python Code

To eliminate the "luck" of a small dataset, K-Fold ensures every data row is used for validation exactly once. We divide the rows into k folds (here, $k=4$).
